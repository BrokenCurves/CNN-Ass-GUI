Recyclables: This includes paper, plastic, glass, metal, and fabric. Recyclables can be reprocessed to reduce pollution and save resources. For example, recycling paper saves wood, recycling plastic yields secondary raw materials, and recycling metals reduces air and water pollution.

Food Waste: Food waste mainly includes food scraps, fruit peels, vegetable leaves, and other organic waste. This type of waste can be composted to produce fertilizer or energy.

Other Waste: Also known as dry waste, this includes bricks, tiles, ceramics, dirt, bathroom waste paper, tissues, and other hard-to-recycle materials. These can be disposed of by sanitary landfill to reduce environmental pollution.

Hazardous Waste: Hazardous waste includes discarded batteries, fluorescent tubes, chemicals, and other materials harmful to humans and the environment. Such waste requires special handling to prevent environmental damage.
